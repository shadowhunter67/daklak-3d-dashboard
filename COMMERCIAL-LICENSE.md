# Commercial licensing

Starting from the commit immediately after the `mit-final-1.0.0` tag, this
repository is released under the Source-Available Evaluation License (see
`LICENSE`), not an open-source license. That license permits viewing,
cloning, local/private evaluation, learning, and non-commercial testing —
it does **not** permit commercial use, production deployment, hosting it as
a service, resale, sublicensing, white-labeling, use in paid client work or
procurement responses, building a competing product from it, or
redistribution.

If your intended use falls outside what `LICENSE` Section 2 permits, you
need a separate written commercial agreement with the Licensor first.

SaaS, resale, white-label, procurement/tender, etc.), expected scale, and
timeline. The Licensor will respond with terms, pricing (if applicable), or
a decline — no commercial license is granted implicitly by opening an
inquiry or by silence.

## What a commercial agreement typically covers

This will vary per engagement, but expect it to address: scope of
permitted use, term, fees (if any), support expectations (or explicit lack
thereof), attribution requirements, and whether modifications may be kept
private or must be contributed back. None of this is pre-agreed by this
document — it is negotiated per license.

## Not legal advice

This page describes a process, not a binding offer or a legal opinion. Any
resulting agreement should be reviewed by your own legal counsel before
signing.
